Biography

Hi, I'm Onyinye Ibiam, a first semester student in the School of Engineering at AltSchool Africa pursuing a diploma in Cloud Engineering. I'm switching careeers from Microbiology to Tech. Microbes are cool, so are packet data - maybe even cooler.

Ever since I can remember, I've always had a particular fascination about technology, especially in the domain of computers. Currently, I'm learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript - I call them "The Three Muskerteers." I'd like to say I'm passionate about learning to code, but lately coding seems to be passionate about me - IYKYK. We're just gonna hang in there.

Outside of anything related to tech, I enjoy music (classical, Trance, rock...), documentaries (nature, history, science...), and looking at all sorts of clouds in the sky (when I get the chance) - I don't think people look at clouds as much as they need to.

Why I Joined Alt School

Well, first of all, I just want to say I didn't join AltSchool Africa because of what Falz the Bahd Guy said in the video ad. Just wanna put it out there, so you know ;). If you're curious, you can watch the video over here.

Anyways, why did I join AltSchool Africa? I joined AltSchool Africa because I saw it as the opportunity I needed to help me switch my career path. The tech world is incredibly vast - to the point that it literally stupefies you. Knowing how to navigate it and take the right turns is crucial in ensuring you don't end up in weird place.

As an individual, you can change careers on your own. But when there's the opportunity to do so, coupled with the help and guidance, you should take that opportunity. I believe that's what AltSchool Africa offers. Plus, there's strength in numbers. I get to meet other cool people on the same journey as I am. It doesn't any better than that. The only way is up!

My Goal for School of Engineering Programme

My goal is pretty simple and straight-forward. I aim to make the best out of this opportunity to learn the skills, mindset, and disposition required to succeed in Cloud Engineering and the tech industry in general.

I might revisit this goal as some point in the future, but for now it is what I'm working with.
